APPROACH

I met with the V.P. of marketing at OC Music and dance and discussed the needs of their current website. Their main goal was to redesign their current website, and find a way to increase the number of student enrollment at the school.

We visited some of their locations to observe and interview some customers as well talk to their employees. We also set up a questionnaire for people to fill out regarding their experiences at Jamba Juice.

INSIGHT AND PAINPOINTS

After researching, we organized all of the painpoints into categories, and discussed possible solutions. We also discussed solutions that were possible as UX designers without completely changing their business model.


  • Most customers were families with kids, or college-aged students with their friends.
  • Wait times for a drink were too long in the stores.
  • There were not enough seating areas for customers in their stores.
  • Ordering a drink online was frustrating because they couldn't do it on their phone.
  • Customers did not want to download the app.
  • 80% of customers interviewed were unhappy with their online ordering system.
  • Most of the customers (over 80%) were not regulars.

SYNTHESIS

After gathering information, researching, and interviewing customers, we concluded that our assumption that the non-responsive site needed to be redesigned was correct. From there, I was able to put together a User Persona, and Storyboard and solution.

After finishing her history exam, Miranda decides to splurge on a drink at Jamba Juice. Since she is busy, she decides to pre-order her drink. After browsing through the menu, she decides she wants the Mango-a-Go-Go smoothie.

To her dismay, she's unable to order a drink from the menu. When she figures out where to order, she realizes the page isn't responsive. She refuses to add anymore apps to her phone-its already too cluttered. Frustrated, she settles for Starbucks instead.

Two weeks later, she still wants her Mango-a-Go-Go smoothie. She checks online once more, to see if she can try to figure out how to pre-order it online.

When she checks their website, she is delighted to see that they have re-designed the site, making it mobile friendly. The process is simple, interactive, and she is excited.

She selects her drink, customizes her options, and chooses her location within minutes. Her order is confirmed.

She arrives at Jamba Juice, bypassing the line, and is pleased that she saved time and was able to get her drink fix.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

"Jamba Juice’s online ordering process is not responsive- making it impossible to pre-order a drink on their customers' phones- resulting in a decrease in sales. "

Busy college students and parents with kids don’t have the time or patience to get in front of a computer to order a drink ahead of time.

While app is available to download, most customers don’t download it because:

  • They don’t want to clutter the interface on their phone
  • They don’t visit often enough to feel a need to download the app.


    • SOLUTION

    Design a responsive, quick, and easy user-flow for ordering from their website.

    Process

    We started with the user flow, discussing the possible ways our customer could order from the website.

    At the same time, we also re-vamped the website's style, discussing what we liked about the current site, and what needed to be updated. We decided to make the website look cleaner and simpler. We also aimed to keep the bright and playfulness of the current website, without looking too juvenile.

    We went from quick rough sketches, to low fidelity wireframing of layout options for the landing page, ordering format, and information page.

    After finalizing out low-fidelity wireframes, we make our digital mockups of the user flow.
